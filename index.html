I. Transportation in Living Organisms

Plants transport water through root pressure and transpiration pull. Food prepared in leaves is transported by translocation, an energy-requiring process.

II. Excretion in Living Organisms

Harmful wastes are removed to maintain health. Humans use kidneys and nephrons; plants excrete by diffusion or storage.

III. Co-ordination in Plants

Plants respond to stimuli through tropic movements and plant hormones such as auxin and abscisic acid.

IV. Nervous System in Humans

Neurons transmit impulses through CNS, PNS, and ANS. Reflex actions provide quick, involuntary responses.

V. Chemical Co-ordination

Endocrine glands release hormones that regulate growth, metabolism, stress response, and reproduction.
